Abstract

Efficient and robust initialization is critical for visual-inertial odometry (VIO), yet many existing methods rely on point-wise visual observations and require iterative maintenance of high-dimensional 3D landmarks, which limits both efficiency and scalability. We propose AVC-Init, a decoupled VIO initialization framework based on Aggregated Visual Constraints (AVC). Using a rotationâ€“translation decoupling strategy, AVC-Init first estimates gyroscope bias via aggregated rotational constraints between co-visible keyframes and pre-integration merging. It then robustly estimates initial velocity, gravity, and accelerometer bias through global position optimization, supporting both loosely-coupled and tightly-coupled schemes. For joint refinement, we introduce Aggregated Epipolar Adjustment (AEA), which decouples observations from states to enable efficient full-parameter estimation in a low-dimensional space without explicitly maintaining 3D points. A depth-weighted multi-view triangulation step further ensures fast, unbiased 3D point generation. Experiments on simulation, EuRoC, and real-world datasets demonstrate that AVC-Init achieves superior accuracy and efficiency.

Experimental Results

Experiments on the EuRoC MAV dataset and simulated sequences show that AVC-Init provides fast convergence and strong estimation accuracy. In particular, using long-baseline aggregated constraints improves the robustness of gyroscope-bias estimation under increasing pixel noise, while the global-position estimators provide accurate and stable recovery of velocity, gravity direction, and scale across a wide range of observation conditions.
