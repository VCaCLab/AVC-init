Abstract

[cite_start]Efficient and robust initialization is critical for visual-inertial odometry (VIO)[cite: 5]. [cite_start]We propose AVC-Init, a decoupled initialization framework based on Aggregated Visual Constraints (AVC)[cite: 7, 101]. [cite_start]By adopting a rotation-translation decoupling strategy, the framework first estimates gyroscope bias using aggregated rotational constraints between co-visible keyframes and pre-integration merging[cite: 8, 104]. [cite_start]Subsequently, initial velocity and gravity are robustly estimated via global position optimization, supporting both loosely-coupled (GP-L) and tightly-coupled (GP-T) schemes[cite: 9, 107]. [cite_start]For joint optimization, we introduce Aggregated Epipolar Adjustment (AEA)[cite: 10, 108]. [cite_start]By decoupling visual observations from states, AEA enables efficient full-parameter estimation in a low-dimensional space without explicitly maintaining 3D points[cite: 10, 109].

Experimental Results

[cite_start]Evaluations on simulation, EuRoC, and real-world datasets demonstrate the superior accuracy and efficiency of AVC-Init[cite: 12]. [cite_start]The long-baseline aggregated constraints provide larger effective parallax, geometrically mitigating the negative impact of pixel noise on gyroscope bias estimation compared to standard adjacent-frame methods[cite: 421].
