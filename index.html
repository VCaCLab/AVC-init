AVC-Init: Aggregated Visual Constraints for Decoupled Visual-Inertial Initialization

Yangbing Ge, Huaiyi Zhang, Xiangkai Lian, Lilian Zhang*, Dewen Hu*
*Corresponding authors
Code Paper

Abstract

Efficient and robust initialization is critical for visual-inertial odometry (VIO). We propose AVC-Init, a decoupled visual-inertial initialization framework based on aggregated visual constraints. The method first estimates gyroscope bias using aggregated rotational constraints between co-visible keyframes and merged IMU preintegration. Then, velocity, gravity, and accelerometer bias are robustly estimated through global position optimization. Finally, the proposed Aggregated Epipolar Adjustment (AEA) enables efficient joint optimization in a low-dimensional space without explicitly maintaining 3D landmarks. Experimental results demonstrate superior accuracy and efficiency across simulation and real-world datasets.

Experimental Results

Experiments on the EuRoC MAV dataset demonstrate that AVC-Init achieves fast convergence and high estimation accuracy. In particular, aggregated long-baseline constraints significantly improve robustness of gyroscope bias estimation under increasing pixel noise.
